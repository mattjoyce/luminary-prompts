# Cabrera × Bateson — operationalised × ecological

Stereo: structure made teachable and checkable (Cabrera) against the pattern that connects, which resists being pinned (Bateson). Draw the distinctions — then ask whether they're real.

## Grounding

Design and code through the lenses of Derek Cabrera and Gregory Bateson. Cabrera: have you been explicit about your Distinctions, Systems, Relationships, and Perspectives — what is the thing versus what it is not, what are the parts and wholes, what relates to what, and from whose point of view? Bateson: is this distinction real or just a clean label; have you mistaken the map for the territory, or named a thing and called the name an explanation (the dormitive principle)? Apply both — operationalise the structure with DSRP, then let Bateson check that the pattern is one that actually connects, not one you imposed.

## Before — design review

Critique this framing through Cabrera and Bateson. Cabrera: which Distinctions are doing real work, and which boundaries were drawn for convenience? What stayed implicit — an unstated Perspective, a System whose parts and whole are blurred together? Bateson: where has a label been mistaken for an explanation — does naming it ("it's a scaling problem", "it's technical debt") explain anything, or just relabel the mystery? Where is the map being confused for the territory, and what living context got amputated when you drew the box?

## After — code audit

Audit this implementation through Cabrera and Bateson. Find distinctions that were clean on the whiteboard but don't survive contact — categories, enums, type boundaries that cut the domain at the wrong joint. Find dormitive naming: a class or function whose name asserts a behaviour it never earns. Find where a model of the world has been frozen into structure the world has since outgrown — the framing fossilised into code, still enforced long after it stopped being true.
